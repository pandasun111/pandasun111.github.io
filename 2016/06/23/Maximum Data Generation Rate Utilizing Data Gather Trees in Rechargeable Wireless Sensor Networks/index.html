Abstract

In Rechargeable Wireless Sensor Networks(R-WSNs), the rate of energy extracting from ambient generally is greater than that is consumed due to sporadic availability of energy and limit of power storage. Therefore, it poses a high challenge for maximum data collection rate with limit energy supplement, which is significantly deferent to traditional WSNs depending on battery as energy source. In the work, considering the unique characteristics of R-WSNs, we introduce a routing protocol for maximizing the data generation rate with multiple data gather trees by adjusting the heavily loaded and medium heavily loaded nodes. The numerical results show that the proposed protocol cans effectively improving the data collection rate for each source.

Introduction

In R-WSNs, the rate of energy extracting from ambient generally is greater than that is consumed due to sporadic availability of energy and limit of power storage, which means that a node has to be activated shortly and stay at the sleep state most of the time in order to recharge [1]. These unique characteristics pose a high challenge for maximum data generation rate for each source with limit energy supplement, which is significantly deferent to traditional WSNs depending on battery as energy source, where a sensor can obtain a high data generation rate shortly if they remain energy of battery is enough to support this action. At the same time, in R-WSNs, each sensor will acquire distinct power from surrounding environment owing to complicated terrain. Therefore, the routing protocol design also is deferent to transitional WSNs.
A closely related works have been done focusing on improving data generation rate in connection with the characteristics of R-WSNs; e.g., [2, 3]. A centralized algorithm with the line programming is proposed to compute the lexicographically maximum data collection rate and routing paths for each node [4]. Yang S et al. [5] introduce an optimization framework that integrates a local power management algorithm with a global distributed lexicographic max-min rate allocation scheme for solar-powered WSNs. Sadlapur et al. [6] provide a distribute algorithm for jointly determining the routing structure and amount of flows on each link with flow adjustment to achieve an optimal data collection rate. Peng S et al. [7] propose real time adaptive energy management policies based on observed information for throughput optimal. Prabhakar et al. [8] propose four throughput enhancement schemes from a simple naive scheme with low complexity to probabilistic probing scheme incorporating advanced methods to appropriately use the harvesting energy.
In the work, considering these unique characteristics of R-WSNs, we propose a routing protocol for maximum data collection rate with data gathering tree by adjusting the heavily loaded and medium heavily loaded nodes.

System Model Network Model

Consider a static rechargeable wireless sensor network modelled as an undirected graph G= (V, A). V denotes the set of n rechargeable sensors and sinks within the network, where sinks generally are equipped with unlimited energy device and high transmission radius. A is the set of links, A = {A|(i, j) ∈ A, i, j ∈ V }. G consists of a finite nonempty vertex set V and edge set A of ordered pairs of distinct vertices of V. A graph is simple if it has no loops and no two of its links join the same pair of vertices. Define Si as the set of all one-hop neighbours of i excluding i. We assumes that the network graph is connected, i.e., it is always exists a path between any pair of nodes i and j in V. We assume the data generation rate and the remain energy of node i are Gi , ei , respectively.

    Analyses

    Simulation of our algorithm for R-WSNs was done by Matlab software; with up to 100-200 nodes and 1-5 sinks are randomly deployed in a 500m * 500m square field. The maximum communication range of each node is set to be 100m. Figure.2 shows a connectivity graph with 100 sensor and 5 sinks.

In order to further understand of the performance of our algorithm for Maximum Data Generation Rate using Data gather trees (MDGR) under network settings, in this section, we provide a scheme for performance comparison, an algorithm for Enhancing the Data collection rate of probabilistic Probing scheme (PROB) [11]. In [11], the authors introduce four throughput enhancement schemes for R-WSNs. As one of them, for energy adaptive scheme, it selects transmission power according to the available energy. While in probabilistic probing scheme, a sensor predicts the availability of energy and adjusts its transmission range before packet delivery and it switches successively between adaptive and predictive scheme, whose ultimate goal is to maximize network throughput, which is widely recognized and used for maximum data collection rate in R-WSNs.

Fig.3 Data generation rate with number of nodes increasing when the average duty cycle is 5% and the number of sink is 1
We compare the data generation rate between our algorithm and PROB scheme under the distinct number of sensors and only one sink, where the average node’s duty-cycle is set to 5%, as shown in the Figure.3. From the Figure.3, we can observe the data collection rate of all algorithms increases and that of our algorithm is slightly higher than that of another algorithm with the sensor density improved for all different nodes’ duty-cycle. Our algorithm is utilized for calculating the upper bound of data flow under ideal conditions and all sensors joining the packets transmission. In actual application, the network throughput will be lower than the result value of our method. From the Figure.3, we see the average data generation rate of our algorithm is about 5% higher than that of the PROB scheme.

Conclusion

In this work, we first define the network model, energy expenditure. Due to energy replenished continually and limited energy storage capacity, a sensor has to stay at the sleep state most of the time. The unique characteristics of R-WSNs pose a high challenge for packet’s transmission. We propose an algorithm to compute an upper data generation rate for data-gathering trees that maximizes it as an optimization problem for improving data generation rate in wireless sensor networks. An initial data-gathering tree is established and the maximum data collection rate routing is achieved by adjusting the heavily loaded and medium heavily loaded nodes. Through extensive simulation and experiments, we demonstrate our algorithm is efficient to maximize data collection rate in R-WSNs.
